# Your first campaign

A complete walk-through from an empty server to a table ready to play. Assumes
Campfire is [installed](installation.md) and running.

## 1. Light the fire (create the admin)

Open Campfire in a browser. The first visit shows **Light the fire** — a one-time
setup screen. Enter a username and password. This account is the **server admin**;
it will also be your first DM, but those two roles are independent (see
[Roles](roles.md)). You're now signed in, looking at the **campaign hub**.

## 2. Install a rule system (optional but recommended)

For a searchable rules compendium — monsters to drop into fights, spells and
conditions to look up — install a rule pack first:

1. **Admin** (top bar) → **Rule systems**.
2. Under *Install from Open5e*, tick the sections you want (Conditions and Monsters
   are the most useful at the table) and click **Install**.
3. Conditions import in a couple of seconds; the full monster list takes about a
   minute — you can keep building while it runs.

!!! note "No rule pack? Still fine."
    The campaign wizard offers a **None / homebrew** option, and you can type
    monsters in by hand during combat. The compendium just makes prep faster.

## 3. Create the campaign

On the hub, click **New campaign**. The wizard has two steps — **Details** (name +
optional description) and **Rule system** (pick your pack, or *None / homebrew*).
Finish and you land on the **campaign dashboard** as its **DM**. Creating a campaign
always makes you its DM — no admin powers required.

## 4. Get your players in

Players need an account, then a seat at your campaign.

- **Accounts** — either a server admin creates them under **Admin → Users**
  (username + a starting password to share), or the DM sends an **invite link**
  from **Members → Invite** and players create their own account when they open it.
- **Seats** — on the campaign open **Members → Add member**, search a username, pick
  a role (**player** / **viewer**), and optionally link them to a character.

Players sign in and see your campaign on their hub.

## 5. Session zero — the charter & characters

Before the dice come out, set the table's expectations. Open **Session zero** and fill
in the **charter**: **lines** (hard limits — content that never appears), **veils**
(soft limits — kept off-screen), the **safety tools** you'll use (X-Card, Open Door…),
any **house rules**, and the **tone** you're playing in. The DM edits it; the whole
table can read it.

Then each player opens **Party → New character**: name, species, class, level, stats,
HP, an optional portrait and bio — or **import a public D&D Beyond sheet** by pasting
its id or URL. As DM you can create or edit any character; players edit only their own,
and a player can keep **more than one** (a backup PC, a familiar).

## 6. Prep the world

From the dashboard (as DM) you build:

- **Quests** — objectives, subquests, a giver NPC, a reward, and a **DM-only secret**
  players never see.
- **NPCs** — role, disposition, home location, their own DM secret.
- **Locations** — drop pins on the map; upload a real map image and place pins on it;
  set discovery (unexplored → explored → current).

Everything is markdown, and DM secrets are stripped server-side — players genuinely
can't see them.

## 7. Ready to play

That's a table ready for session one. Next:
**[Running a game](../guides/running-a-game.md)** walks the actual session.
